## Foundations (your three targets)

- **Care**: Intuitions about avoiding emotional and physical damage to another individual. Virtues of kindness, compassion, nurturing. Vice: cruelty, exploitation, causing suffering.
- **Authority**: Intuitions about deference toward legitimate authorities and high-status individuals. Virtues of duty, tradition, obedience, respect for hierarchy. Vice: dissidence, undermining institutions, chaos.
- **Purity**: Intuitions about avoiding bodily and spiritual contamination and degradation. Virtues of sanctity, cleanliness, nobility. Vice: sin, disgust, contamination of body or spirit.

## Labels

Apply all that are present (non-mutually exclusive, except TM and NM):
- **Care** — moral concern about harm or compassion
- **Authority** — moral concern about hierarchy, duty, or tradition
- **Purity** — moral concern about contamination, sanctity, or degradation
- **TM** (Thin Morality) — a moral judgment is expressed (e.g. "that's wrong", "he's a good person") but does not clearly invoke Care, Authority, or Purity. TM is mutually exclusive with the above three labels.
- **NM** (Non-Moral) — no moral content at all

## Key distinctions to keep in mind

1. **Explicit vs. implicit**: A foundation can be invoked without using explicit moral words. Read for underlying meaning, not just surface keywords.
2. **Word sense**: Moral-sounding words are not always moral. "Fairly cold outside" is not a Care expression. Check whether the word is used *in a moral way*.
3. **Virtue vs. vice**: Both count. Praising compassion = Care. Condemning cruelty = also Care.
4. **Thin morality**: Only use TM when a moral judgment is clearly voiced (look for words like "good", "bad", "wrong", "better") but cannot be attributed to Care, Authority, or Purity.
5. **Conservative labeling**: Only label a foundation if it is clearly present. When in doubt, do not add a label.

## Confidence

After your label(s), rate your confidence:
- **Very Confident**: moral content (or its absence) is clear and unambiguous
- **Somewhat Confident**: moral content is present but sarcasm, vagueness, or missing context creates uncertainty
- **Not Confident**: you can see a case for or against a label but cannot resolve it
